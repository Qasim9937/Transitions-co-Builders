Why co-building?

Real-world challenges are complex, interconnected and wicked. No individual or actor whether in the public or private space, can single-handedly construct or deploy a solution to a complex structural challenge – especially one that is interconnected like the unresolved youth question in Africa

Across Africa and beyond, some of the most catalytic ventures have not emerged from lone innovators or traditional top-down institutions, but from the collaborative ambition of intrapreneurs, mission-driven individuals embedded within systems who mobilize their expertise, time, networks, and influence to co-create solutions that are both impactful and commercially viable. These intrapreneurs do more than build programs; they build systems, often in informal or under-resourced environments, by leveraging shared values, mutual accountability, and a spirit of co-ownership.

The .transitions Co-Builders Fellowship draws on this tested model of entrepreneurial collaboration. It recognizes that no single individual or actor whether in the public or private space , can single-handedly construct or deploy a solution to a complex structural challenge – especially one that is interconnected like youth unemployment in Africa.

What is therefore needed is a new organizing structure approach - that brings together strategically positioned individuals, each contributing distinct forms of skills, expertise, knowledge, experince, on-the-ground intelligence, and distributed networks—toward a common goal of building bankabale, impact-driven, scalable and opportunity-enabling systems.

This Fellowship is therefore a co-creation platform grounded in the principles of collaborative leadership, distributed ownership, and ecosystem-level accountability. Its foundation is built on the proven experience of co-builders who know how to navigate complexity, align unlikely allies, and translate bold ideas into ventures that deliver both purpose and performance.
