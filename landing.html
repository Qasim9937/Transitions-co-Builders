Who are the
co-Builders?

The .transitions Co-Builders Fellowship is a co-creation platform grounded in the principles of collaborative leadership, distributed ownership, and ecosystem-level accountability. Its foundation is built on the proven experience of co-builders who know how to navigate complexity, align unlikely allies, and translate bold ideas into ventures that deliver both purpose and performance

Vision & Problem Context

TAYARi.Transitions is building Africa’s premier platform for future-ready talent. We exist to close the gap between education and opportunity and unlock Africa’s next generation of builders.

Problems

  • Growing Skills Mismatch
  • Untapped demographic dividend & risks associated with rising unemployment
  • Untapped innovation potential of young Africans
  • High stakes of the learning-to-earning transition
  • Brain Drain
  • Systemic Barriers

The .transitions co-Builders Fellowship is designed to equip these intrapreneurs with the tools, networks, and collaborative platforms needed to scale their impact. By fostering a culture of shared ownership and systems thinking, we enable a new generation of builders to design resilient ventures and unlock Africa’s next transformation.

The Co-Builder
Model & Charter

The Co-Builder Model defines how individuals and institutions collaborate to build shared systems with clarity and commitment. It outlines a distributed model of responsibility, transparent governance, and co-ownership.

Principles include inclusive leadership, shared accountability, and an emphasis on enabling builders to co-create with agency while staying aligned to a common mission.

Operational Model

The goal of the Operational Model is to enable co-builders to build autonomously with inter-connected mandates while ensuring timely, measurable delivery of outcomes across all domains.

The Secretariat

While co-builders bring diverse expertise, time, networks and leadership across the various domains, the Secretariat ensures alignment, continuity, and delivery across all moving parts. It holds the center, ensuring that the diverse experts within the transitions Co-Builders ecosystem are moving in sync, guided by shared values, evidence, and purpose. It translates collective ambition into coordinated momentum.

Collaborative Leadership

Breakthrough impact is only possible when actors with diverse capabilities co-create across boundaries. Collaborative leadership nurtures humility, trust, and a shared commitment to building what matters—together.

Distributed Ownership

Power is shared, not centralized. Co-builders take ownership of outcomes within their domains—stewarding impact with autonomy, accountability, and alignment to shared values and vision.

Ecosystem-Level Accountability

Lasting change requires alignment beyond organizations. We hold ourselves accountable to ecosystems—communities we serve, futures we shape, and systems we seek to transform.

Frequently Asked Questions

The Fellowship is a co-creation platform designed to nurture collaborative leadership, distributed ownership, and ecosystem-level accountability to solve Africa’s most complex challenges through talent incubation and venture building.

We support ventures and initiatives that address youth employability, entrepreneurship, innovation, and systemic challenges in Africa, focusing on solutions that are both impactful and commercially viable.

While the Fellowship focuses on Africa’s demographic transition and talent ecosystem, it welcomes global collaborators aligned with our mission and values.

The Fellowship is grounded in three key principles: Collaborative Leadership, Distributed Ownership, and Ecosystem-Level Accountability. These guide how we build ventures and support systemic impact.
